Shelly Sterling stated she will fight to keep her share of the Los Angeles Clippers and plans to eventually divorce Donald Sterling [doc0_0,doc1_0,doc1_5,doc1_17]. She emphasized that her stake in the team should be separate from her husband’s actions, saying, “I don’t know why I should be punished for what his actions were” [doc1_6,doc1_13]. The NBA banned Donald Sterling for life and fined him $2.5 million following racist comments, urging owners to force him to sell the team [doc1_4,doc1_12]. Sterling’s lawyer, Pierce O’Donnell, disputed the NBA’s interpretation of its constitution, arguing California law and the U.S. Constitution override the league’s rules [doc1_9,doc1_10]. The NBA stated league rules would terminate all owners’ interests if a controlling owner’s stake is removed by a 3/4 vote [doc1_7,doc1_8]. Sterling mentioned financial considerations delayed her divorce, with her financial advisor advising against proceeding immediately [doc0_3,doc1_15,doc1_16]. She also suggested Donald Sterling may be experiencing “the onset of dementia” [doc1_18]. Celebrity figures like Oprah Winfrey and Magic Johnson have expressed willingness to buy the team, though Donald Sterling remains the owner, barred from team operations [doc1_19]. Richard Parsons was named interim CEO of the Clippers, overseeing daily operations while ownership issues remain unresolved [doc1_20]. Sterling described the Clippers as her “passion” and “legacy to my family,” having been involved with the team for 33 years [doc1_2,doc1_3].
